# OctoAcme Project Management: Docs Overview

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management Docs! This collection captures our core project management processes, workflow templates, and role definitions. Use these resources to ensure consistent, high-quality delivery across teams, smooth onboarding, and accelerated improvements.

## Process Summary

OctoAcme's project management framework operates through a structured five-phase lifecycle designed to balance iterative delivery with clear accountability and measurable outcomes:

**Initiation** validates business need and aligns stakeholders around a clear problem statement and success criteria through a lightweight Project One-pager. **Planning** breaks approved work into shippable increments with defined acceptance criteria, dependencies mapped, and resources allocated. **Execution** focuses on day-to-day delivery with a regular communication cadence—daily standups, weekly PM-PdM syncs, and twice-weekly delivery standups—ensuring transparency and fast blocke escalation. **Release** standardizes deployment to production with pre-flight checks including passing CI/security scans, prepared smoke tests, and documented rollback plans. Finally, **Close & Retrospective** captures learnings through structured retrospectives that convert insights into actionable improvement items with named owners.

The framework emphasizes three core enablers across all phases: **Clear Roles & Ownership** (Project Manager coordinates delivery and manages risks; Product Manager defines outcomes and measures success; Developers implement and collaborate on design; QA ensures quality) ensure accountability. **Lean, Repeatable Artifacts** (Project One-pager, Risk Register, Definition of Done, Acceptance Criteria) keep teams aligned without heavy documentation. **Data-Informed Decisions** through velocity tracking, success metrics, and continuous measurement drive evidence-based prioritization and improvement. Together, these practices create a culture of psychological safety, fast feedback, and continuous refinement—turning team knowledge into documented, scalable processes that accelerate onboarding and reduce single-person dependency risk.
